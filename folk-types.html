Chapter Three

The Folk Types

Each Folk Type has five SFX. During Pathways you choose two: one at Stage 1 and one at Stage 5. The remaining three can be unlocked through growth.

SFX Are Not Permissions

A Folk character can attempt anything that fits their nature using the normal rules. A Mage without Rend can still try to conjure a fireball; they just have to roll to create it as an asset like anything else. What an SFX does is skip that work. The SFX trades a cost (a die in the pool, a Plot Point, stepped-up stress) for bypassing the normal process. If your character doesn't have an SFX for something, they can still try it. It just takes more effort.

Vampire

The Vampire d8

You are an undead creature of the night, sustained by blood and centuries of survival. You live among the Folk and the human world alike, older than most things around you and hungrier than you let on.

SFX: Beyond Mortality

Add a d8 to the Scene Pool when you perform a supernatural feat of strength or speed. Gain a d8 asset for the rest of the scene.

SFX: Compulsion

Spend a Plot Point to double your Mask die in your dice pool when attempting to influence or convince someone.

SFX: The Feeding

Spend a Plot Point to step down stress on one attribute of your choice when you drink blood.

SFX: Ancient Weakness

Gain a Plot Point when you choose to fail a test involving one of your vulnerabilities. If you are in a contest involving your vulnerability and fail, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Flesh attribute.

SFX: Blood Sustains

When you feed during a recovery scene, step down stress on two attributes instead of one.

Limit: Children of the Night

When exposed to direct sunlight, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Flesh attribute by one, starting at d6. If this steps up past d12 you are Stricken and take Flesh Trauma.

Werewolf

The Werewolf d8

You are something between human and beast, carrying the wolf inside you every moment of every day. You shift when you choose, not when the moon demands it, and that control is everything — because losing it costs more than you can afford.

SFX: The Beast Rises

Add a d8 to the Scene Pool to shift into your bipedal wolf form. Gain a d8 asset for the rest of the scene representing your transformed state.

SFX: Keen Senses

Spend a Plot Point to use your heightened smell or hearing to identify a creature, object, location, or event and notice up to three important details about them.

SFX: Hunter's Eye

Spend a Plot Point to add a d6 asset representing a read on a target's weaknesses or intentions after observing them for at least one exchange.

SFX: That Actually Hurts

Gain a Plot Point when you choose to fail a test involving silver or one of your vulnerabilities. If you are in a contest involving your vulnerability and fail, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Flesh attribute.

SFX: Shake It Off

When you achieve a heroic success, instead of stepping up your effect die you may step down stress on one attribute.

Limit: Old Weakness

When exposed to silver or another chosen vulnerability, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Flesh attribute by one, starting at d6. If this steps up past d12 you are Stricken and take Flesh Trauma.

Fae

The Fae d8

You are a creature of the ancient world, older than the cities that surround you and bound by rules most Folk have forgotten. You look almost human. Almost. The pointed ears give you away to those who know what to look for, though most humans see what they expect to see. You keep your word. Not because anyone makes you. Because breaking it would make you something you are not.

SFX: Glamour

Add a d8 to the Scene Pool to make one object or creature appear, sound, and seem like something else of similar size. Create a d8 asset representing the glamour for the rest of the scene.

SFX: Enchantments and Curses

Spend a Plot Point to give an object or creature an Enchanted d8 asset or Cursed d8 complication for the duration of the scene.

SFX: Ancient Tongue

Spend a Plot Point to communicate with any living thing — plant, animal, or spirit — for the duration of the scene. Add a d6 to the Scene Pool as the veil between worlds shifts.

SFX: Fae Revealed

Gain a Plot Point when you choose to fail a test involving your vulnerability to iron or cold iron. If you are in a contest involving your vulnerability and fail, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Flesh attribute.

SFX: Otherworldly Grace

When you take stress to your Will attribute, you may spend a Plot Point to step it down before applying it.

Limit: Iron Aversion

When exposed to iron or another chosen vulnerability, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Flesh attribute by one, starting at d6. If this steps up past d12 you are Stricken and take Flesh Trauma.

Mage

The Mage d8

You understand something most Folk only sense vaguely: that the world has rules, and those rules can be bent. Not broken. The universe always collects what it is owed, and you have learned to pay the price before it comes to collect. Most of your magic takes preparation, components, and time. Some of it doesn't. All of it costs something.

SFX: Forge

Inflict d8 stress on your Will attribute to create, mend, or conjure something through magic. Create a d8 asset representing what you've made for the rest of the scene.

SFX: Rend

Add a d8 to the Scene Pool to destroy, damage, or unmake something through magic. Create a d8 asset or inflict a d8 complication on a target representing the destructive effect.

SFX: Arcane Eye

Spend a Plot Point to perceive hidden truths, read magical signatures, or glimpse past or future events. Create a d8 asset representing what you've learned for the rest of the scene.

SFX: Burn Bright

Gain a Plot Point when you choose to fail a magical test because the cost is too high. If you are in a contest involving magic and fail, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Will attribute.

SFX: The Work Sustains

When you successfully complete a ritual during a recovery scene, step down stress on two attributes instead of one.

Limit: Equivalent Exchange

Both 1s and 2s count as hitches when rolling your Lineage Distinction. 2s may still be kept in totals and as effect dice.

Sidebar: Which Realm Applies?

Mage magic divides into three realms, each with its own cost and purpose.

Forge is constructive magic: creating something that wasn't there, mending something broken, conjuring a material effect into existence. Healing a wound, raising a barrier, crafting an enchanted object, summoning light. If you're making or restoring something, this is the realm. The cost is Will stress, paid upfront.

Rend is destructive magic: tearing, breaking, unmaking. Blasting a door off its hinges, disrupting an enchantment, collapsing a structure, severing a magical bond. If you're destroying or damaging something, this is the realm. The cost lands on the scene, not you: a d8 added to the Scene Pool.

Arcane Eye is perceptive magic: reading what can't be read by normal senses. Identifying a magical signature, scrying a location, reading the echoes of past events, sensing what's hidden. If you're learning something through magic, this is the realm. The cost is a Plot Point.

When an action spans two realms, pick the one that better reflects the primary intent. If it's genuinely split, the GM can help.

Revenant

The Revenant d8

You are a ghost who refused to let go — not of life exactly, but of presence. You found a body, took it, and made it yours. The original soul is gone. What remains is you, wearing borrowed flesh in a world that doesn't know the difference. The body decays slowly. You don't think about it. You have other things to worry about.

SFX: Slip Through

Add a d8 to the Scene Pool to become temporarily intangible. Create a d8 asset representing your incorporeal state for the rest of the scene or until you choose to return to flesh.

SFX: Whisper

Add a d8 to the Scene Pool to briefly inhabit and influence a minor GMC for the rest of the scene. The GMC acts strangely but doesn't fully lose control.

SFX: Echoes of the Flesh

Spend a Plot Point to access a faint residual memory from your corpse's original owner. Create a d8 asset representing what you learn from the echo.

SFX: Unmoored

Gain a Plot Point when you choose to fail a test because your connection to your corpse weakens at a critical moment. If you are in a contest involving your ghostly abilities and fail, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Will attribute.

SFX: Death's Remove

When you achieve a heroic success using your ghostly abilities, instead of stepping up your effect die you may step down stress on one attribute.

Limit: Untethered

When circumstances force you out of your corpse or threaten your connection to it, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Will attribute by one, starting at d6. If this steps up past d12 you are Stricken and take Will Trauma.

Sidebar: Whisper and Player Consent

The Whisper SFX allows a Revenant to inhabit and influence another character. When that character is a GMC, the GM controls how this plays out. But some tables use named GMCs that individual players feel strong ownership over, and some GMs populate their scenes with characters that carry emotional weight for specific players.

Before using Whisper on any named character, check in with your GM and other players. A quick out-of-character note keeps the table aligned and prevents the SFX from landing in a way nobody wanted. The mechanical cost is already there. The social cost shouldn't be a surprise.

Golem

The Golem d8

You were made, not born. A Mage built you, stitched you together, and breathed magic into the seams. You don't age. You don't hunger. You don't feel pain. What you feel instead is harder to name: an awareness of the craft that made you, a sense of the magic running through everything around you, and the quiet knowledge that you will outlast almost everyone you meet.

SFX: Sense the Craft

Spend a Plot Point to detect and identify magical workings, enchantments, or Mage-craft in the scene. Create a d8 asset from what you sense.

SFX: Tireless

Spend a Plot Point to remove a complication caused by exhaustion, hunger, thirst, or any biological need. You simply don't have those needs.

SFX: Endure

When you would be taken out, spend a Plot Point to step down the stress that caused it and remain in the scene instead.

SFX: Flaw in the Design

Gain a Plot Point when you choose to fail a test because a fundamental limitation of your constructed nature prevents you. If you are in a contest involving your physical capabilities and fail, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Flesh attribute.

SFX: Stillness of Stone

When you achieve a heroic success using your physical capabilities, instead of stepping up your effect die you may step down stress on one attribute.

Limit: Unraveling

When exposed to significant anti-magic, dispelling effects, or forces that disrupt magical bindings, gain a Plot Point and step up stress on your Flesh attribute by one, starting at d6. If this steps up past d12 you are Stricken and take Flesh Trauma.
