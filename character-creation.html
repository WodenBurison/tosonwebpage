Character Creation

All players build their characters simultaneously across five stages, each contributing to a shared mind map and the character sheet at the same time.

Character Creation at a Glance

Before Pathways begins, choose your Folk Type. You'll select your starting SFX during Stages 1 and 5.

Stage Name Type Key Gains
1 The Lineage Major 2 elements, The Lineage distinction, 1 SFX, 2 role step-ups, 1 R/R advancement
2 The Waking Standard 1 element, 1 attribute step-up, 1 role step-up, 2 R/R advancements
3 The Breaking Meta 1 element + meta action, 1 attribute step-up, 3 role step-ups, 1 R/R advancement, The Root distinction, your Limit
4 The Ground Standard 1 element, 1 attribute step-up, 1 role step-up, 2 R/R advancements
5 The Folk Major 2 elements, The Core distinction, 1 SFX, 2 role step-ups, 1 R/R advancement

All characters also carry the Hidden Nature Limit.

Totals across all five stages:

7 minimum elements · 7 R/R advancements · 4 Attribute step-ups · 9 Role step-ups

2 SFX + 1 Limit selected · Attributes start at d6 · Roles start at d4

The Mind Map

Character creation in TOSON happens collectively. All players build their characters at the same table (or the same document), working through five stages together. Each stage adds to a shared mind map and to your character sheet at the same time.

The mind map is a visual record of every character's connections: the people they know, the places that matter, the other Folk in the room. As each player adds elements and draws connections, a picture of the Folk community emerges before anyone has made a single roll. By the time the last stage is complete, the players haven't just built characters. They've built a neighborhood.

For in-person play, a large sheet of paper works. For online play, any shared whiteboard tool will do: Miro, MindMeister, and FigJam are all solid options.

Element Types

The mind map uses four shapes:

Shape Represents
Square Player characters. Each player draws their own character as a square at the start of play.
Circle People. Any named NPC: a family member, a Folk neighbor, a Watch council member, a hunter with a face and a name.
Diamond Locations. Places that matter: a neighborhood, a community center, a Vanguard regional office, a Folk-run business.
Triangle Factions and organizations. The Watch. A Balance circle. A Van Helsing Society chapter. Any institution with enough reach to act as a unit.

Connections

Every connection requires context. When you draw a line between any two elements, write a brief note on or beside that line explaining why the connection exists. Raised me after my parents disappeared. Owes me a favor I haven't called in. The place I go when I need to disappear. It doesn't need to be long. It needs to be specific enough that everyone at the table understands the relationship.

After writing the context, decide what the connection means mechanically. Take the element, if you directly connected to it, as either a Relationship or a Resource:

Type Starting Value When to Use
Relationship d6 A personal bond. Typically people (circles or squares).
Resource 2d4 Access rather than attachment. Typically locations (diamonds), factions (triangles), or people you have a useful connection to rather than a close bond with.

The Pathway moves through five stages in order. Don't read ahead. Each stage is designed to be answered in the moment, with what you know at that point in the story.

Here's the shape of the whole arc: The Lineage establishes your heritage — your family, your community, and what being Folk meant in the world you grew up in. The Waking covers who you became outside of that: your own relationships, your own ambitions, the Peace becoming something you personally had to live inside. The Breaking is the moment you lost control — the event that put you on the Watch List. The Ground is the aftermath: who stayed, what you built, the life you've been living since. The Folk is who you are now, in the wider community, shaped by all of it.

Stage One

The Lineage

Major Stage

You didn't become Folk. You were born one. This stage begins before you could make choices about it: your family, your community, and the inheritance that came with being what you are. Every Folk character carries something forward from the people who raised them, whether that's a strong sense of identity, a complicated relationship with the Peace, or a gap where something should have been explained and wasn't. The Lineage stage asks you to locate your character in that inheritance: not just what you are, but who taught you what it means, and what piece of the world they handed you.

  1. Who in your family shaped how you understand what you are, and what did they teach you?
  2. What place did you grow up in, and what does it still mean to you?
Mind Map — Add two new elements, connect both to your character.

Stage Two

The Waking

Standard Stage

Before the Breaking, there was this. The Waking is the stage of becoming your own person — of the Folk world coming into focus on your terms, not your family's. The Peace stops being something you were told about and becomes something you actually have to navigate: the rules of it, the politics of it, the way it shapes every interaction between Folk and the human world. This stage is also about what you wanted before everything changed. What you were building toward. The person you were in the process of becoming when the Breaking interrupted.

  1. Who outside your family became important to you as you grew into your own person?
  2. What did you want for yourself before everything changed?
  3. What did you learn about the Peace that your family hadn't prepared you for?

Stage Three

The Breaking

Meta Stage

Every player character in TOSON is Marked, and this is where that happened. The Breaking is the fixed center of the Pathway — the stage everything before it was building toward and everything after it responds to. Something went wrong. You lost control in a way that couldn't be quietly undone, and the Watch List exists because of moments exactly like this one. This stage doesn't ask whether it happened; it asks what it cost, who was there, and what you carried out of it that you can't put down.

The meta action here reflects that weight. The Breaking doesn't just change you. It changes the shape of the world around you.

  1. What happened: what did you lose control of, and who was there?
  2. What did it cost you, and is that debt settled?
  3. What do you know now that you wish you could unknow?
Mind Map — Add one new element, then take one meta action:
· Draw a connection between any two non-character elements and define their relationship
· Sever one of your existing connections and replace it with a new element
· Introduce an element for another player's character, and they define their own connection to it

Stage Four

The Ground

Standard Stage

After the Breaking, you found your footing. Maybe slowly, maybe with help. The Ground is about the life you built in the aftermath: the people who stayed, the places that became yours, the obligations that anchor you to where you are now. Being Marked doesn't end your story; it reorients it. This stage asks what your new normal looks like — not before, not the moment of crisis, but the quieter and more complicated reality of living as someone the Watch has noticed.

  1. Who do you rely on now — Folk, human, or both?
  2. What place in the city has become yours since the Breaking?
  3. What obligation keeps you here when leaving would be easier?

Stage Five

The Folk

Major Stage

By the time you reach this stage, the character you are now has been shaped by everything that came before: lineage, growth, crisis, and recovery. The Folk stage asks who that person is in the wider community. Not just how others see you, but where you stand — on the Peace, on what being Marked means for what you want, on the kind of Folk you've chosen to be. The Core distinction you choose here isn't inherited or assigned by history. It's the identity you've arrived at.

  1. What do the Folk in your community know you for?
  2. Where do you stand on the Peace now, given everything that's happened to you?
  3. What do you want that being Marked makes harder to have?
