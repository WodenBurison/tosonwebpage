The hunter's name was Aldrich, which was probably not the name on the business card she'd handed him. The card said Vanguard Global Security, Regional Threat Assessment, and a phone number with a city code two states over.

She had arrived at nine in the morning, which told him she'd been watching the office long enough to know his hours. She sat across from his desk with her coat still on and both hands visible on the table, which was the kind of courtesy that meant she was very confident she didn't need them yet.

"You've been asking questions," she said.

Emil set down his coffee. "I'm a private investigator. It's the work."

"About the Whitfield case."

"Client confidentiality."

She nodded like she'd expected that. "James Whitfield hired you. His brother died eight months ago. Natural causes. Cardiac event, no suspicious circumstances. The case is closed."

"Then there's nothing to investigate."

"And yet." She slid a photograph across the desk.

Emil looked at the photographs without touching them.

"Mr. Cross. How long have you been in the city?"

It was a simple question. It was not a simple question.

She reached into her jacket.

Emil went through the wall.

About This Book

The Other Side of Normal is a Cortex Prime supplement for urban fantasy play. It gives you a setting, characters to play, and a particular kind of story: Folk trying to hold their lives together in a world that would end them if it knew what they were.

The Folk are monsters. Vampires, werewolves, fae, mages, revenants, golems, and others besides. They live in cities, work jobs, keep apartments, have complicated feelings about their families. Most humans don't know they exist. After World War II, an unspoken agreement called the Peace ended the hunting of Folk. A council called the Watch (half human, half Folk) keeps the Watch List, which tracks Folk who threaten the Peace or risk exposing the hidden world. The system isn't perfect. It's just what everyone agreed to instead of more war.

Player characters are Folk navigating that world. The interesting stories here are political: who owes what to whom, what the Peace costs to maintain, and whether the thing you're protecting is worth the cost. Not every session needs to be heavy. But the weight is available whenever you reach for it.

This supplement was designed for play-by-post: asynchronous text play through forums or chat platforms. Every decision in this book was made with that format first. It works for live play too, but PbP is what it was built for.

What You Need to Play

This supplement assumes you already know how to play a TTRPG and are familiar with Cortex Prime. You can find the base rules at cortexrpg.com. The video by The Dungeon Newb's Guide is a solid introduction — search "how to play Cortex Prime" on YouTube, or watch it here.
